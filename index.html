02

Yin

Slow Meditative Deep Tissue

In Yin class, the muscles stay cool and remain relaxed. Poses are held for two to five minutes at a time in silence and stillness, giving the fascia a massage, stimulating adaptive stress in ligaments, and hydrating joints.

Not only does it perfectly complement yang classes and lifestyle, but it also engages the parasympathetic nervous system and may allow emotional release, ultimately helping reduce chronic stress.

03

Yoga Nidra

Restorative Guided 1:1 Available

Nidra means "sleep" in Sanskrit, but the goal of yoga nidra is NOT to fall asleep. It is a dreamlike state, otherwise known as a hypnagogic state — a place between wakefulness and sleep. It is believed that here you can subconsciously plant intentions, or sankalpa, for the future.

It is a practice of pratyahara, or turning the senses inward. All you have to do is get cozy in savasana and listen. Benefits include improved sleep, emotional resilience, and support for anxiety and pain management.

About

A teacher,
a student always

My path in yoga began at 12 years old and has been woven into every chapter of my life since. I came to teaching through a deep personal practice and a belief that the mat is one of the most honest mirrors we have.

By day I teach high school Psychology in Madrid — a fitting parallel, since yoga and psychology are both, at their core, the study of how human beings work. I teach yoga with the same curiosity I bring to the classroom: always asking why, always looking underneath.

My classes blend the intelligence of vinyasa with the depth of yin holds, the science of pranayama, and the profound stillness of yoga nidra. I teach in English and Spanish and welcome all levels, all bodies, all chapters of life.

I'm based in Madrid and teach with the Madrid Yoga Collective and in collaboration with Ferne & Fels.
